The Three Up-Regulating Factors

(Wake the system up — without whipping it into chaos)

These arise when the system is under-energized:

sloth, torpor, depressive mud, collapse, freeze.

They raise gain just enough to bring the system back online.

2) Investigation (Dhamma-vicaya) — Debug Mode

Investigation is not rumination.
It’s curiosity applied to the field.

Mechanically, it:

brightens discrimination

increases usable cognitive traction

sharpens perceptual resolution

collapses volitional doubt

turns vague fog into crisp data

gives the cursor something real to grip


Instead of:

> “Oh no, everything might be happening,”



Investigation says:

> “What exactly is happening here — right now — in this frame?”



This is the factor that directly counters doubt:
it shrinks the decision tree until volition can move again.


---

3) Energy (Viriya) — The Up-Shift Circuit

Energy restores upward momentum when the system drops into:

sloth/torpor

low-charge collapse

freeze states

emotional exhaustion


Energy is not frantic intensity.

Energy is aliveness.

Mechanically, it:

raises baseline activation

brings alertness back online

improves cursor responsiveness

lifts the field out of dullness

restores “forward motion” without strain


This is the factor that pulls you out of low-voltage states.

4) Joy / Rapture (Pīti) — The “Progress Detected” Signal

Joy often arises when:

the system is stable enough,
pressure is low enough,
and the mind stops treating itself as a threat.

Joy is the nervous system saying:

> “Oh. I can be alive without danger.”



Mechanically, it:

brightens the field

makes attention buoyant

dissolves dullness

supports early unification

supplies the “reward tone” that helps First/Second Jhāna stabilize


Together, Investigation + Energy + Joy are your up-regulating trio:

they lift the system out of collapse, numbness, and depressive drift.

The Three Down-Regulating Factors

(Turn the gain down — without losing clarity)

These arise when there is too much charge:

restlessness, anxiety, over-amped joy, overclocked cognition.

They lower gain and smooth the field.

5) Calm / Tranquility (Passaddhi) — The Volume Knob

Calm is not dullness.

Calm is:

smooth breath

relaxed musculature

reduced micro-tension

lowered sympathetic charge

a quiet, stable body


When calm is active:

the pixel-field stops buzzing

restlessness loses fuel

worry has less traction


Joy lifts you out of collapse.

Calm prevents you from launching into chaos.

Calm is the down-shift that supports the slide from bright pīti into softer sukha and neutrality.

6) Concentration (Samādhi) — Cursor Stability in a Quiet Field

Samādhi is not hypnosis or trance.

> Samādhi = cursor stability in a quiet field.



Mechanically, it:

lets the cursor rest where you place it

keeps memory-delta tracking cleanly

prevents fragmentation under load


Concentration:

stabilizes First Jhāna (steady focus + pleasant tone)

stabilizes Fourth (uniform, equanimous field)


Samādhi is viewport lock: the system stays coherent while gain turns down.

7) Equanimity (Upekkhā) — The Drain Channel

Equanimity is not apathy.

> Equanimity is the ability to let the wave complete without clamping.



Mechanically, it:

drops gain without dulling awareness

returns valence toward neutral

keeps the cursor unlatched even with strong content

lets formations run their full cycle and drain

prevents craving from closing its jaws


Equanimity is NEITHER at scale:
no push, no pull — complete permission for patterns to rise, peak, and fade.

This down-regulator:

balances desire and ill will

softens restlessness and worry

provides the neutrality of Fourth Jhāna

supports the later step-downs toward formless absorption and cessation
